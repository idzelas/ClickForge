The geometry recomputation in the fidget toy studio is too slow — complex shapes take 15-20 seconds to respond to any slider change, and even simple shapes feel sluggish. Fix this with the following changes:
1. Web Worker for geometry computation
Create src/lib/geometryWorker.ts and move createOuterShellGeometries, createInnerClickerGeometries, and createColorLayerGeometries into it. The worker receives plain settings and serialized SVG point arrays (no THREE objects), and posts back raw Float32Array position/normal buffers. The main thread reconstructs BufferGeometry from those using BufferAttribute. While the worker is busy, keep the previous geometry visible on the canvas — don't blank it. Show a small loading indicator on the viewport instead. This is the most critical fix because right now every slider drag freezes the entire UI thread.
2. Reduce polygon sampling from 128 to 48 points
In fidgetGeometry.ts and polygonOffset.ts, change every getPoints(128) call to getPoints(48). The findFirstCrossing function in polygonOffset.ts is O(n²) per pass and runs O(n) passes, so 128 points means roughly 128³ operations worst-case. 48 points cuts that by ~18x with no visible quality difference for typical SVG outlines. For the final ExtrudeGeometry calls only, use 64 to keep face edges smooth.
3. Add Douglas-Peucker simplification after transformToMm
In fidgetGeometry.ts, after transformToMm converts the SVG path to mm coordinates, run a Douglas-Peucker simplification on the resulting point array with a tolerance of 0.15mm before passing it to insetPolygon or outsetPolygon. This removes redundant near-collinear points that are especially common in SVGs traced from raster images, reducing the polygon offset workload significantly.
4. Split geometry memos into shape and extrusion phases
In fidgetGeometry.ts, separate the work into two stages. The first stage — transformToMm, offsetShapeInward, offsetShapeOutward — only depends on targetSizeMm, lockDimension, insetAmount, clearanceMm, mirrorShell, and svgIsClickerShape. The second stage — the actual ExtrudeGeometry calls — depends on the first stage's output plus the depth and pocket settings. In Studio.tsx, make these two separate useMemo calls. This means dragging shellSolidFloor, shellWallExtension, or keycapPocketDepth only reruns the cheap extrusion step, not the expensive polygon offset.
5. Add commitOnRelease to all remaining sliders
In Studio.tsx, these SliderRow components are missing commitOnRelease and trigger a full geometry rebuild on every animation frame while dragging: pocketOffsetX, pocketOffsetY, colorLayerThickness, and clearanceMm. Add commitOnRelease to all of them.
